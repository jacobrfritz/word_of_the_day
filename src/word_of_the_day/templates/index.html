How word. Works

Every day, word. selects a vocabulary word designed to challenge, inspire, or occasionally make you look up its pronunciation three times.

The journey begins with Ingestion, where a background scheduler fetches raw text corpora from diverse digital channels—ranging from Wikipedia and classic Project Gutenberg novels to Substack feeds—alongside previously cached database words.

Next, candidates undergo Filter & Rank. To ensure reusability, a 365-day check filters out recently used selections. The remaining words are lemmatized and checked against a Part-of-Speech and length gate. Candidates are also filtered by a Zipf frequency "goldilocks" range to exclude overly common or obscure words. Survivors are ranked: either via term frequency (TF-IDF) across documents, Zipf frequency scoring to target rare vocabulary, or using a SentenceTransformer to compute cosine similarity against a daily rotating target cluster centroid (curated via K-Means and the Elbow Method).

Highly ranked candidates are then passed to Validation. Here, the system lazily queries the dictionary API to verify the word exists and retrieve its definition. Because API calls are rate-limited, validation is done on-demand, stopping the moment we find enough valid words to satisfy our candidate pool limit.

Finally, instead of a rigid argmax, the pipeline applies Softmax Selection scaled by a tunable temperature parameter. High temperatures distribute selection probability more evenly to foster variety and creativity, while low temperatures collapse the choice deterministically onto the highest-scoring candidate.

The chosen word is then saved to the database, delivered via email daily newsletter at 6:00 AM, and served via our FastAPI backend.
