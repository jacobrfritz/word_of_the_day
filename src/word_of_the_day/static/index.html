How word Works

Every day, word. selects a vocabulary word designed to challenge, inspire, or occasionally make you look up its pronunciation three times.

The journey begins with Ingestion, where a background scheduler fetches raw text corpora from diverse digital channels—ranging from Wikipedia and classic Project Gutenberg novels to Substack feeds.

To prevent repeat performances, these raw texts are immediately run through a 365-Day Reusability Check, instantly filtering out any word that was selected as a word of the day in the past year.

Next, candidates face the Zipf Frequency Filter. This acts as a 'goldilocks' check: weeding out overly common conversational filler (looking at you, the), and discarding obscure OCR noise and scanner typos.

The survivors are validated via the Free Dictionary API to verify they are real English words with actual definitions and etymologies. No made-up jargon allowed.

Finally, the remaining candidates enter the Semantic Ranking Pipeline. A SentenceTransformer model encodes candidate words and compares their embeddings against a curated seed database. Words are scored by the average cosine similarity of their K-nearest neighbors, sorting them by how well they match a sophisticated, literary vibe.

The highest-ranking candidate is crowned the Word of the Day, saved to our SQLite database, and served via FastAPI to your screen.
